About Me

Interest: Machine Learning, Image Processing, and Deep Learning

I am working with Matthew Lease towards my dissertation on Provable, Low-weights and Interpretable Neural Newtorks for general purpose Machine Learning models related to Information Retrival and Data Compression systems.
I am a part of the Information Retrieval and Crowdsourcing Lab.

Previously, I worked on Denoising and Feature Selection for Hyperspectral Images.
I completed my Masters in Image Processing and Vision (EE) from IIT Kharagpur, under Jayanta Mukhopadhyay and Ritwik Layek on Computational model for Image Saliency.

Featured Work: 1

High-Precision, Low-weight, Fully Interpretable, Neural SVD solver

A two stage neural optimization approach as an alternative to conventional and randomized SVD techniques, where the memory requirement depends explicitly on the feature dimension and desired rank, independent of the sample size. The network minimization problem converges to a low rank approximation with high precision. Our architecture is fully interpretable where all the network outputs and weights have a specific meaning.

Featured Work: 2

A Low-weight, Interpretable Neural net for approximating the weak Pareto optimal manifold. A Pareto filter to extract the strong Pareto set.

Our proposed hybrid approach arrives at the true Strong Pareto set with high density. It is scalable to large dimensions where current state of the art approaches takes hours.
